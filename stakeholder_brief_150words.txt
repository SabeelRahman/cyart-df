CONFIDENTIAL - MANAGEMENT BRIEFING
Case: WEEK3-TRAINING | Prepared by: Syed Sabeelur Rahman | Date: 2026-03-25

1. Executive Summary
I conducted a forensic investigation into a simulated insider threat incident. The analysis confirmed suspicious activity involving unauthorized data access and malware deployment on a employee workstation.

2. Key Findings
• Data Access: Sensitive files (budget reports, credentials) were accessed during a narrow time window.
• Device Connection: A USB flash drive was connected coinciding with file access, suggesting potential data exfiltration.
• Malware Presence: Malicious software was identified that establishes persistent access and communicates with external servers.

3. Actions Taken
• I preserved all digital evidence with cryptographic hash verification to ensure legal admissibility.
• I documented the chain of custody throughout the investigation.
• I extracted technical indicators (IPs, domains, file hashes) for blocking.

4. Recommendations
Based on my analysis, I recommend reviewing access controls for sensitive financial documents and enhancing monitoring for unusual USB device usage. I also suggest deploying detection rules for the identified malware behaviors across the enterprise.

5. Impact
This was a training exercise, but the techniques used reflect real-world incident response procedures.
